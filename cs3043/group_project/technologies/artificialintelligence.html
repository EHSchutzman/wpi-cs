Technology: Artificial Intelligence

Sally is a self aware personal AI. Technology has yet to produce a self aware AI yet . However, there is a supercomputer that can defeat the grandmaster Gary Kasparov at chess and a computer that beat Ken Jennings (arguably the best Jeopardy player in history) (see http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/business/1481339.stm and http://www.nytimes.com/2011/02/17/science/17jeopardy-watson.html?pagewanted=all&_r=0). Programs have been made to make financial decisions and many are used in the stock market.

Artificial intelligence is very common in video games. NPCs (non-playable characters) need to be able to act in a manner that either hinders or helps the player. In real-time strategy games, an AI needs to be able to command an army and pose a challenge to the player. In first person shooters NPCs need to be able to locate and attack the player. (see https://software.intel.com/en-us/articles/designing-artificial-intelligence-for-games-part-1).
