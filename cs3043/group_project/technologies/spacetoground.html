Technology: Space to Ground Communications

Communications between the ground and space is quite developed today. One common example is the advertisements for satellite TV and internet that can be found on television networks. A more advanced example, showing people on the ground communicating with people in space is the NASA "Tracking and Data Relay" Satellite Fleet (see: http://www.nasa.gov/). This network of satellites is used to keep in contact with people in orbit and has virtually 100% area coverage. This fleet has been operational for quite awhile, with the youngest being sent up in 2002, and the oldest during 1983 (see: http://www.universetoday.com/101196/how-do-astronauts-on-the-space-station-stay-in-touch-with-earth/). The continued use of the network shows that it is a stable and reliable communication method that may continue to be used for many years to come.
