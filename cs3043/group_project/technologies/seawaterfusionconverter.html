Technology: Seawater to Fusion Energy Converter

The Tet uses the fusion energy converter to generate energy from the Earth for its own purposes. The fusion energy converter is a futuristic technology. Currently, we can harness the kinetic energy of seawater and convert it into energy that can be stored/used for electricity generation. (Source: http://www.google.com/patents/US4446378)

Humanity has used water to power equipment for centuries - running water was harnessed to power mills in the early 11th century. Growth in technology has improved that process - in 2000, the first large scale plant for generating energy from waves was built in Scotland. (Source: http://www.worldoceanobservatory.org/events/oceanenergy/current3.htm)

There have also been recent breakthroughs in nuclear fusion (see: http://rt.com/usa/fusion-energy-power-ignition-806/), but large scale nuclear fusion power plants are still some years away according to the most optimistic estimates (see: http://www.iter.org/proj/iterandbeyond).
